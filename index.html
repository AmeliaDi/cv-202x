EN / ES

I live between two languages — the one with only 0s and 1s, and the one that paints worlds on a canvas.

My work spans the entire stack from bare-metal firmware to quantum circuit design. I write kernels, compilers, cryptographic engines, and neural networks in Assembly — not because I have to, but because I find beauty in the constraints.

At the same time, I publish research on prime numbers in complex planes and propose quantum circuits that evaluate Wilson's Theorem in superposition. Math, to me, is another kind of art.

I believe the most interesting things happen at the edges — between disciplines, between abstraction levels, between the poetic and the precise.

Función de Brighid M(z) — Quantum Computing Domain

Extension of the Brighid function toward quantum computation. Proposes a reversible logic gate circuit to evaluate Wilson's Theorem in superposition. Coupling the Brighid Oracle with Grover's algorithm and Quantum Phase Estimation provides a theoretical framework to extract the n-th prime — bypassing classical combinatorial explosion.
