Shadow Libraries ↗

I live between two languages — the one with only 0s and 1s, and the one that paints worlds on a canvas.

My work spans the entire stack from bare-metal firmware to some art design. I write kernels, compilers, cryptographic engines, and neural networks in Assembly — not because I have to, but because I find beauty in the constraints.

At the same time, I publish research on prime numbers in complex planes and propose quantum circuits that evaluate Wilson's Theorem in superposition. Math, to me, is another kind of art.

I believe the most interesting things happen at the edges — between disciplines, between abstraction levels, between the poetic and the precise.

Outside code and research, I'm an activist — currently participating in Shadow Libraries, a project for radical open access to knowledge.
